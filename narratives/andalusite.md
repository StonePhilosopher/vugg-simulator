## blurb

Al₂SiO₅, orthorhombic — coarse, near-SQUARE {110} prisms (the two cleavages meet at ~89°). The low-pressure member of the aluminosilicate trio: kyanite holds the high pressures, sillimanite the high temperatures, and the three meet at the triple point near 500°C and 0.4 GPa. Andalusite is the index mineral of a rock baked at low pressure — a contact aureole around a shallow intrusion.

## variant: chiastolite

Chiastolite — the cross stone. The host metapelite carried reduced organic carbon, and as the andalusite porphyroblast grew, the rapidly-advancing corners of the square prism swept that graphite into their growth sectors faster than the flat faces did. Each corner sector built up a dark diagonal arm; a section cut across the c-axis shows the four arms as a Maltese cross. This is growth-SECTOR zoning — composition partitioned by which face was growing — not the concentric banding of a changing fluid. The mechanism is Dowty's (1976) protosite model; the quartz-and-graphite co-precipitation was pinned by Mason and co-workers (2010, Gondwana Research) in the Zhoukoudian aureole.

## variant: viridine

Viridine — the manganese-rich green andalusite, Mn³⁺ substituting into the aluminium site deeply enough to turn the crystal green rather than the usual rose.

## variant: pink

Pink-rose, from a trace of Mn³⁺ in the lattice — the common colour of clean andalusite in a Mn-poor pelite.

## closing

A diagnostic crystal: where it grows, the rock stayed below the kyanite field — low pressure, high heat, the signature of contact metamorphism rather than deep burial.
