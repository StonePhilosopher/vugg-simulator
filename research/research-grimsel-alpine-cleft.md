## 1. Setting
Host: Variscan (~299–295 Ma) Central Aar Granite / Grimsel Granodiorite —
peraluminous, K-feldspar-rich, biotite-bearing granite supplying Si, K, Na, Al,
Ca, Fe, Ti, F. Zerrklüfte = extensional tension clefts opening normal to local
extension during late-Alpine collision + retrograde exhumation; they RE-OPEN
episodically (each re-opening = fresh fluid pulse + new crystal generation — the
crack-seal spine).

**The event spine (Gnos, Mullis, Bergemann et al. 2025, Swiss J. Geosci.
118(1):12 ✅):** D1 reverse faulting ~14.6 Ma @ **450 °C / 440 MPa**; D2 reverse→
dextral ~12–11 Ma @ **385–375 °C / 320–330 MPa**; D3 strike-slip reactivation
~10–7 Ma @ **330 °C / 230 MPa → 300 °C / 240 MPa**; D4 brittle ~3.3 Ma (cataclasis).
One Zinggenstock cleft records SIX fluid-inclusion populations (Episodes of fissure
formation in the Alps 2021, Swiss J. Geosci. 114, doi:10.1186/s00015-021-00391-9 ✅)
— multiple re-openings in one cleft.

## 2. Fluid (broth + retrograde curve)
Canonical: **Mullis, Dubessy, Poty & O'Neil 1994, GCA 58(10):2239–2267 ✅**
(volume/pages ADS-verified). Aar/Grimsel sits in the **H₂O-dominant → CO₂-bearing
epizone** regime (NOT the reducing CH₄ zone to the north). Quartz nucleates
~450–550 °C, grows down the retrograde path, **stops ~200 °C**, at **0.3–0.45 GPa
(3–4.5 kbar)**, ~13–18 km, over 5–7 Myr (Mullis 1996 ⚠️ + IGC synthesis ✅;
cleft-specific path Gnos 2025 ✅).

**Recommended sim curve:** start ~450 °C / 4.4 kbar → monotonic cool to ~200 °C /
2.4 kbar. (Deep-Tessin variant could start ~500 °C.)

- Salinity LOW ~1–3 wt% NaCl eq (dilute meteoric-metamorphic — the defining
  contrast with MVT/evaporite brines).
- pH near-neutral→mildly alkaline ~6–8 (inferred from adularia+calcite+quartz; not
  a single measured value — flagged).
- Redox OXIDIZING (hematite "iron roses" = Fe³⁺ present → O₂ moderate). Contrast the
  CH₄-reducing northern zone (which would give pyrite/chlorite only).

Order-of-magnitude broth (host-buffered granite fluid): Si saturating (high, drops
with T — the retrograde-solubility growth driver); Na tens–low-hundreds; K tens
(adularia-buffered); Ca tens; Al low but ESSENTIAL (smoky color + adularia); Fe
low–moderate (hematite/chlorite); F low (fluorite/apatite/biotite); CO₂/CO₃ present
(>10 mol% volatile in epizone — calcite + Tessin driver); Ti trace (titanite).
Driver of crystallization = retrograde cooling lowering solubility; CO₂ pulses
modulate habit.

## 3. Paragenesis (early high-T → late low-T)
1. **Adularia** (low-T hydrothermal K-feldspar) — often the substrate. Diagnostic.
2. **Chlorite** — green, as included films/needles in quartz + coatings (gives the
   green phantoms). Early–mid.
3. **Smoky quartz** — volumetric main stage ~450→200 °C. Tessin habit if deep/hot/
   CO₂-rich; prismatic if shallower/cooler.
4. **Hematite "iron roses" (Eisenrosen)** — Fe³⁺ rosettes, oxidizing marker, mid.
5. **Titanite (sphene)** — wedge crystals, LATE on quartz, low-T fissure phase
   (≤~400 °C). Göschener Alp = type-quality alpine titanite (Handbook ✅).
6. **Apatite** — late, low-T.
7. **Fluorite** (pink/octahedral) — late, low-T.
8. **Calcite** — latest, lowest-T lining.
9. Rarer Ti-oxides: rutile, anatase, brookite (low-T alpine accessories).

Fissure monazite begins ≤~380–400 °C (towards END of quartz growth ✅) → late/cool
assemblage = monazite+titanite+apatite+calcite; main/early = smoky qz+chlorite+
adularia; hematite straddles.

**Smoky color:** Al³⁺→Si⁴⁺ substitution forming [AlO₄]⁻ + charge-compensator
(H/Li/Na); natural γ-irradiation (U/Th/⁴⁰K over Ma) → [AlO₄]⁰ hole color center →
smoky/morion. **Rossman 1994, Rev. Mineral. 29:433–467 (MSA) ✅** (primary). "Rink"
= general quartz radiation dosimetry, NOT alpine-specific — flagged, don't cite for
alpine smoky color.

**Field discriminators:**
- Aar QUARTZ cleft (granite host): smoky qz + adularia + chlorite + hematite ±
  titanite/fluorite/apatite/calcite; dilute low-sal fluid; retrograde ≤450 °C;
  free-grown druse.
- Pegmatite: magmatic melt, graphic granite, beryl/tourmaline, >600 °C, no adularia/
  chlorite-smoky/druse.
- Epidote cleft (Tormiq, amphibolite host): epidote+clinozoisite+zoisite+green
  Cr-titanite+byssolite+diopside — the Ca-Fe-Al mafic-host suite. The discriminator
  is the HOST-rock signature carried into the cleft.

## 4. Quartz habits + conditions (the crux for the morphology arc)
- **TESSIN habit** (steep rhombohedral, prism m strongly reduced, tapers to tip;
  "Penninic"): higher T (~500 °C) + CO₂-rich + SLOW growth (macromosaic internal
  structure). → sim: high-T + high CO₂ + low σ ⇒ Tessin form. Standard prismatic =
  lower-T/lower-CO₂/faster.
- **SCEPTRE** (gen-2 cap WIDER than gen-1 stem): growth interruption + fresh fluid
  pulse, classically crack-seal re-opening; renewed pulse often volatile-enriched
  (CO₂/CH₄ unmixing) → fast growth on tip corners/edges → wider cap, often skeletal
  faces absent on gen-1. Alpine amethyst sceptres: wider cap grew at LOWER T than
  the smoky stem. → sim: re-opening event = pause growth (hiatus) → inject fresh
  CO₂-enriched pulse → spike σ → wider 2nd-gen cap. THIS is the signature the
  census found absent; the scenario must engineer it.
- **FADEN** (white thread): tectonic crack-seal healing, each crack re-healed +
  decorated by a plane of fluid inclusions. Mainly weakly-metamorphosed sediments
  (less typical of granite Aar core but real Alpine-wide). "Rapid crack-seal growth
  of Faden quartz" 2025, J. Struct. Geol. (S0191814125000070) ✅ exists, author/pages
  not fetched ⚠️.
- **GWINDEL** (twisted): cumulative growth-axis rotation in slowly continuously
  opening clefts; exclusive to alpine fissures, rare, always with macromosaic
  normal-habit quartz.
- **WINDOW/SKELETAL/FENSTER**: rapid growth / high σ, often from H₂O–CH₄/CO₂
  unmixing concentrating growth on edges → recessed windows. Same family as the
  sceptre cap.

## 5. Titanite (sphene) CaTiSiO₅ — new mineral entry
Physical data ✅ from Handbook of Mineralogy (Mineral Data Publishing 2001 v1.2;
cites Dana 1892; Deer Howie Zussman 1982 RFM v.1A:443–466; Oberti, Smith, Rossi &
Caucia 1991 Eur. J. Mineral. 3:777–792).
- Formula CaTiSiO₅ (CaTiO[SiO₄]).
- **Monoclinic**, 2/m, space group P2₁/a (A2/a). a=7.057 b=8.707 c=6.555 Å β=113.81°
  Z=4.
- Habit: **wedge-shaped (sphenoid)** flattened ∥{001}, or prismatic along [110];
  to 16 cm; also massive.
- **Twinning: {100} contact AND penetration** (the classic); less commonly lamellar
  {221} (also a parting). ← canonical twin for twin_laws.
- Cleavage good {110}; parting {221}.
- Hardness 5–5.5. Density 3.48–3.60 (calc 3.53).
- Color black/brown/gray/yellow/red/**green**/colorless. Brown-yellow = common
  igneous/metamorphic; **GREEN (Cr-bearing) = prized alpine variety** (Cr→Ti). Luster
  adamantine→resinous; very high dispersion ("fire").
- Cations: **Ca + Ti + Si** (+O). Substitutions Al³⁺/Fe³⁺→Ti⁴⁺ (charge-balanced by
  F⁻/OH⁻→O), minor Nb/REE. **Alpine-cleft titanite is near-end-member, LOW-Al/low-F**
  (Göschener Alp: Ca₁.₀₀(Ti₀.₉₇Fe₀.₀₃)Si₁.₀₀O₄.₉₆ ✅) — discriminator vs high-Al
  metamorphic titanite. → engine: alpine titanite = clean Ca-Ti-Si, low F.
- Formation: magmatic ~690–740 °C; hydrothermal ~250–670 °C; alpine-cleft = LOW-T
  hydrothermal/fissure end, late on quartz (≤~400 °C).
- Field discriminator: adamantine-resinous luster + wedge/sphenoid + high fire +
  monoclinic flattened habit; green=Cr alpine, brown=ordinary.
- Localities (Handbook ✅): Tavetsch; St. Gotthard/Ticino; Zermatt; **Göschener Alp**
  (Aar-adjacent, the reference analysis); Val Maggia/St. Marcel (Italy); Zillertal
  (Austria); Pakistan alpine clefts (green Cr); Dodo mine, Subpolar Urals.
- Association (Handbook ✅): albite, chlorite, epidote, apatite, allanite, monazite,
  magnetite, ilmenite, biotite, diopside, calcite. (chlorite+apatite+calcite overlap
  the Aar suite; epidote+diopside overlap the Tormiq suite → titanite usable in BOTH.)

## 6. Anchor specimen
Matrix of cleft-lining adularia (white-cream blocky rhombic K-feldspar) + dark-green
chlorite druse; lustrous SMOKY QUARTZ — Tessin-habit prisms tapering to sharp points
(deeper/hotter Grimsel) OR smoky SCEPTRES (pale stem, wider darker sometimes-skeletal
cap); green chlorite phantoms/inclusions + films; HEMATITE "iron roses" (metallic
platy rosettes) on quartz/adularia; wedge-shaped green-honey TITANITE among the qz +
chlorite; small apatite prisms; late calcite lining. Free-grown 3-D cavity druse
(not a sawn vein) — the Zerrkluft hallmark.
