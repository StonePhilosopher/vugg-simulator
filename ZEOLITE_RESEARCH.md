# Zeolite Paragenesis in Deccan Traps Basalts

Compiled research on zeolite-group minerals and associated species in the Deccan Traps flood-basalt province and analogous basalt-hosted vug environments.

---

## Natrolite

### Identity
- **Formula:** Na₂Al₂Si₃O₁₀ · 2H₂O
- **Crystal system:** Orthorhombic
- **Mineral group:** Zeolite
- **Hardness (Mohs):** 5–5.5
- **Specific gravity:** 2.2–2.25
- **Cleavage:** Perfect on {110}
- **Fracture:** Brittle, uneven
- **Luster:** Vitreous to silky
- **Color:** Colorless, white, gray, yellowish, pinkish; occasionally greenish with Fe
- **Transparency:** Transparent to translucent
- **Streak:** White
- **Notable visual features:** Slender prismatic or acicular crystals, often radiating sprays or tufts; also columnar, fibrous, or massive.

### Formation Conditions
- **Nucleation temperature range:** ~50–250 °C
- **Optimal growth temperature:** ~100–200 °C
- **Chemistry required:** Na (dominant), Al, Si, H₂O; forms in alkaline, silica-rich fluids
- **Required pH range:** ~9–11
- **Geological environment:** Basalt vesicles and vugs; low-grade metamorphic rocks; alpine clefts.

### Paragenesis
- **Position in sequence:** Stage II (burial metamorphism)
- **Forms BEFORE:** Thomsonite (if Na persists), scolecite (if Ca increases), mesolite (Na–Ca intermediate), and late-stage calcite/apophyllite.
- **Forms AFTER:** Early clays (celadonite, smectite), mordenite.
- **Commonly associated with:** Mordenite, heulandite, stilbite, scolecite, mesolite, calcite, chalcedony, apophyllite.

### Famous Localities
- **Deccan Traps, India:** Widespread in basalt quarries; among the earliest zeolites deposited in Stage II.
- **Franklin, New Jersey, USA:** Acicular white sprays in prehnite–datolite vugs.
- **Pigeon Point, California, USA:** Fibrous and radiating forms in sandstone/conglomerate.

## Thomsonite

### Identity
- **Formula:** NaCa₂Al₅Si₅O₂₀ · 6H₂O
- **Crystal system:** Orthorhombic
- **Mineral group:** Zeolite
- **Hardness (Mohs):** 5–5.5
- **Specific gravity:** 2.3–2.4
- **Cleavage:** Good on {010}, poor on {100}
- **Fracture:** Uneven to subconchoidal
- **Luster:** Vitreous to pearly
- **Color:** White, colorless, pink, green, yellow; commonly exhibits concentric color banding.
- **Transparency:** Transparent to translucent
- **Streak:** White
- **Notable visual features:** Spherical or reniform (kidney-shaped) aggregates with radiating internal structure (“eye” stones); tabular crystals less common.

### Formation Conditions
- **Nucleation temperature range:** ~50–250 °C
- **Optimal growth temperature:** Higher end of zeolite range, reflecting Ca-rich fluids
- **Chemistry required:** Ca and Na in roughly 2:1 ratio, Al, Si
- **Required pH range:** ~8–10
- **Geological environment:** Basalt vesicles; altered volcanic tuffs; occasionally in sedimentary concretions.

### Paragenesis
- **Position in sequence:** Stage II (often earlier than natrolite or mesolite, reflecting hotter, Ca-richer conditions)
- **Forms BEFORE:** Mesolite (cooler, Na-enriched fluids), scolecite (more Ca), stilbite, late calcite/apophyllite.
- **Forms AFTER:** Celadonite, smectite, early chalcedony.
- **Commonly associated with:** Natrolite, mesolite, scolecite, prehnite, calcite, chalcedony, apophyllite.

### Fluorescence
- **Under SW (254 nm):** Variable — some pink/orange tints reported depending on trace Mn or organics.
- **Under LW (365 nm):** Generally weak; banding may appear muted.

### Famous Localities
- **Burhanpur area, Madhya Pradesh, India:** Classic source for orange “ Thompsonite ” (orange thomsonite has not been seen in the market since the 1980s).
- **Lake Superior region (Michigan / Minnesota, USA):** Thomsonite “eye” stones from amygdaloidal basalts; state stone of Minnesota.
- **Deccan Traps quarries:** Present in Stage II vugs alongside natrolite and mesolite.

## Mesolite

### Identity
- **Formula:** Na₂Ca₃Al₈Si₉O₃₀ · 8H₂O
- **Crystal system:** Monoclinic
- **Mineral group:** Zeolite (natrolite subgroup)
- **Hardness (Mohs):** 5
- **Specific gravity:** 2.26
- **Cleavage:** Good on {110}
- **Fracture:** Uneven
- **Luster:** Silky to vitreous
- **Color:** Colorless, white, gray; may show pale tints.
- **Transparency:** Transparent to translucent
- **Streak:** White
- **Notable visual features:** Very slender, hair-like acicular crystals in dense radiating sprays (“spray paint” or “sea urchin” tufts); often overgrows natrolite or thomsonite cores.

### Formation Conditions
- **Nucleation temperature range:** ~50–200 °C
- **Optimal growth temperature:** ~80–150 °C
- **Chemistry required:** Intermediate Na/Ca ratio (between natrolite and scolecite)
- **Required pH range:** ~8–10
- **Geological environment:** Basalt vesicles; most common as an intermediate between natrolite and scolecite as fluids evolve.

### Paragenesis
- **Position in sequence:** Stage II (mid to late Stage II, cooler than thomsonite)
- **Forms BEFORE:** Scolecite (if Ca rises), stilbite, heulandite, apophyllite, late calcite.
- **Forms AFTER:** Thomsonite (hotter, Ca-rich), natrolite (Na-rich).
- **Commonly associated with:** Natrolite, thomsonite, scolecite, stilbite, chalcedony, calcite, apophyllite.

### Fluorescence
- **Under SW (254 nm):** White to pale green-yellow (weak to moderate), from trace uranyl or organics.
- **Under LW (365 nm):** Weaker than SW; may appear chalky white.

### Famous Localities
- **Poona (Pune) / Jalgaon / Nasik areas, India:** Dense mesolite sprays coating stilbite or natrolite in Deccan basalt vugs.
- **Rio Grande do Sul, Brazil:** Radiating sprays on amethyst geodes.
- **Berufjörður, Iceland:** With scolecite and apophyllite.

## Scolecite

### Identity
- **Formula:** CaAl₂Si₃O₁₀ · 3H₂O
- **Crystal system:** Monoclinic
- **Mineral group:** Zeolite (natrolite subgroup)
- **Hardness (Mohs):** 5–5.5
- **Specific gravity:** 2.16–2.40
- **Cleavage:** Perfect on {110}
- **Fracture:** Uneven
- **Luster:** Vitreous to silky
- **Color:** Colorless, white, pinkish, yellowish; may show salmon or lilac tints from trace Fe.
- **Transparency:** Transparent to translucent
- **Streak:** White
- **Notable visual features:** Slender prismatic or acicular crystals in radiating sprays, sheaf-like bundles, or delicate “hair” tufts; larger crystals sometimes have square cross-sections.

### Formation Conditions
- **Nucleation temperature range:** ~50–200 °C
- **Optimal growth temperature:** ~100–150 °C (sometimes slightly hotter than mesolite)
- **Chemistry required:** Ca-dominant, Al, Si
- **Required pH range:** ~7.5–9.5
- **Geological environment:** Basalt vesicles; low-grade metamorphic rocks; cavities in granitic pegmatites (rare).

### Paragenesis
- **Position in sequence:** Stage II (upper/lower zones depending on temperature gradient — generally hotter-deeper)
- **Forms BEFORE:** Stilbite, heulandite, calcite, apophyllite.
- **Forms AFTER:** Natrolite, thomsonite, mesolite.
- **Commonly associated with:** Stilbite, heulandite, apophyllite, calcite, chalcedony, natrolite, mesolite, mordenite.

### Fluorescence
- **Under SW (254 nm):** Creamy white to pale orange (moderate); some specimens show subtle zoning.
- **Under MW/LW (310–365 nm):** Weaker creamy-white glow.
- **Activator:** Trace Mn²⁺, possibly uranyl ion inclusions.

### Famous Localities
- **Nasik / Pune / Jalgaon districts, India:** Classic sprays and sheaves on stilbite matrix; among the most iconic Deccan zeolite specimens.
- **Berufjörður, Iceland:** In basalt with apophyllite and other zeolites.
- **Kongsberg, Norway:** Large crystals in diabase.

## Stilbite

### Identity
- **Formula:** NaCa₄Al₉Si₂₇O₇₂ · 28H₂O (stilbite-Ca) or Na₉Al₉Si₂₇O₇₂ · 28H₂O (stilbite-Na)
- **Crystal system:** Monoclinic
- **Mineral group:** Zeolite (heulandite subgroup)
- **Hardness (Mohs):** 3.5–4
- **Specific gravity:** 2.12–2.22
- **Cleavage:** Perfect on {010}
- **Fracture:** Uneven
- **Luster:** Vitreous to pearly
- **Color:** White, colorless, yellow, pink, peach, orange, green (from trace Fe or Cu)
- **Transparency:** Transparent to translucent
- **Streak:** White
- **Notable visual features:** Distinctive bow-tie or sheaf-like aggregates of tabular crystals (“bow ties” are diagnostic); single crystals are tabular with diamond-shaped cross-sections.

### Formation Conditions
- **Nucleation temperature range:** ~50–200 °C
- **Optimal growth temperature:** ~100–150 °C
- **Chemistry required:** Ca and/or Na, Al, Si, abundant H₂O
- **Required pH range:** ~7.5–9
- **Geological environment:** Basalt vesicles; hydrothermal veins; sedimentary deposits.

### Paragenesis
- **Position in sequence:** Stage II (late Stage II, overlaps with heulandite)
- **Forms BEFORE:** Apophyllite, powellite, late calcite.
- **Forms AFTER:** Scolecite, mesolite, natrolite, thomsonite, mordenite, early calcite.
- **Commonly associated with:** Heulandite, apophyllite, scolecite, calcite, chalcedony, prehnite, chlorite.

### Fluorescence
- **Under SW (254 nm):** Yellow to orange-yellow (moderate to bright); pink/peach varieties may fluoresce warmer tones.
- **Under MW/LW (310–365 nm):** Similar yellow-orange glow; often brighter than heulandite.
- **Activator:** Uranyl ion (UO₂²⁺) substitution, trace organics.

### Famous Localities
- **Pune / Nasik / Jalgaon districts, India:** Peach and white “bow tie” stilbite is arguably the most recognizable Deccan zeolite in the collector market.
- **Faeroe Islands / Iceland:** Colorless to white sheaves in basalt.
- **Nova Scotia, Canada:** Classic North American locality.

## Heulandite

### Identity
- **Formula:** (Na,Ca)₂₋₃Al₃(Al,Si)₂Si₁₃O₃₆ · 12H₂O
- **Crystal system:** Monoclinic
- **Mineral group:** Zeolite (heulandite subgroup)
- **Hardness (Mohs):** 3.5–4
- **Specific gravity:** 2.18–2.22
- **Cleavage:** Perfect on {010}
- **Fracture:** Uneven, brittle
- **Luster:** Vitreous to pearly
- **Color:** White, colorless, yellow, pink, peach, red-brown, green; occasionally apple-green.
- **Transparency:** Transparent to translucent
- **Streak:** White
- **Notable visual features:** Coffin-shaped or rectangular tabular crystals; often flattened on {010}; may form bow-tie clusters similar to stilbite but more angular.

### Formation Conditions
- **Nucleation temperature range:** ~50–200 °C
- **Optimal growth temperature:** ~80–150 °C
- **Chemistry required:** Ca–Na variable, Al, Si
- **Required pH range:** ~7.5–9
- **Geological environment:** Basalt vesicles; altered tuffs; hydrothermal veins; occasionally in sediments.

### Paragenesis
- **Position in sequence:** Stage II (mid to late Stage II; in Deccan localities, heulandite may occupy slightly higher/cooler levels than scolecite)
- **Forms BEFORE:** Apophyllite, powellite, late calcite.
- **Forms AFTER:** Mordenite, scolecite, mesolite, natrolite, thomsonite.
- **Commonly associated with:** Stilbite, apophyllite, scolecite, calcite, chalcedony, mordenite.

### Fluorescence
- **Under SW (254 nm):** Yellow-green to white-green (variable; generally weaker than stilbite).
- **Under MW/LW (310–365 nm):** Weak pale yellow or greenish-white.
- **Activator:** Uranyl ion traces; Mn²⁺ in some varieties.

### Famous Localities
- **Maharashtra, India:** Common in Deccan basalt cavities alongside stilbite and scolecite.
- **Berufjörður, Iceland:** Colorless coffin-shaped crystals.
- **British Columbia / Nova Scotia, Canada:** North American localities.

## Mordenite

### Identity
- **Formula:** (Na₂,K₂,Ca)Al₂Si₁₀O₂₄ · 7H₂O
- **Crystal system:** Orthorhombic
- **Mineral group:** Zeolite
- **Hardness (Mohs):** 3–4
- **Specific gravity:** 2.08–2.15
- **Cleavage:** Good on {010}
- **Fracture:** Uneven
- **Luster:** Silky to vitreous
- **Color:** White, colorless, gray, pink, yellow
- **Transparency:** Translucent to opaque (fibrous forms)
- **Streak:** White
- **Notable visual features:** Fine fibrous or cotton-like aggregates (“wool rock”), rarely distinct prismatic crystals; sheaf-like or radiating tufts when crystalline.

### Formation Conditions
- **Nucleation temperature range:** ~50–250 °C
- **Optimal growth temperature:** ~100–200 °C
- **Chemistry required:** Na–K–Ca variable; high silica
- **Required pH range:** ~8–10
- **Geological environment:** Basalt vesicles; altered volcanic rocks; sedimentary deposits; hydrothermal systems.

### Paragenesis
- **Position in sequence:** Stage II (early to mid Stage II)
- **Forms BEFORE:** Heulandite, stilbite, scolecite, mesolite, apophyllite.
- **Forms AFTER:** Celadonite, smectite, early chalcedony.
- **Commonly associated with:** Heulandite, stilbite, scolecite, chalcedony, calcite, apophyllite.

### Fluorescence
- **Under SW (254 nm):** Variable; fibrous forms may show weak greenish-white.
- **Under MW/LW (310–365 nm):** Generally weak or non-fluorescent.

### Famous Localities
- **Maharashtra, India:** Fibrous aggregates in Deccan basalt vugs.
- **Poona area, India:** Sheaf-like crystalline forms.
- **Nova Scotia / New Brunswick, Canada:** North American occurrences.

## Apophyllite

*Note: Apophyllite is not technically a zeolite, but it is closely associated with zeolite assemblages in basalt vesicles and is frequently grouped with them in trade and museum displays.*

### Identity
- **Formula:** (K,Na)Ca₄Si₈O₂₀(F,OH) · 8H₂O (fluorapophyllite-(K), most common)
- **Crystal system:** Tetragonal
- **Mineral group:** Phyllosilicate (layered silicate)
- **Hardness (Mohs):** 4.5–5
- **Specific gravity:** 2.3–2.4
- **Cleavage:** Perfect on {001} (basal pinacoid)
- **Fracture:** Uneven
- **Luster:** Vitreous to pearly
- **Color:** Colorless, white, green, yellow, pink, violet, brown
- **Transparency:** Transparent to translucent
- **Streak:** White
- **Notable visual features:** Prismatic or tabular tetragonal crystals with pyramidal terminations; often exceptionally gemmy; exhibits anomalous birefringence and rainbow iridescence from internal growth planes.

### Formation Conditions
- **Nucleation temperature range:** 20–150 °C
- **Optimal growth temperature:** 50–100 °C
- **Chemistry required:** Si (high), Ca (moderate), K (moderate to low), F or OH
- **Required pH range:** 7.5–10
- **Geological environment:** Vesicles in basalt and other volcanic rocks; granite pegmatites and skarns (rare).

### Paragenesis
- **Position in sequence:** Stage III (late hydrothermal — LAST major phase)
- **Forms AFTER:** Scolecite, stilbite, heulandite, mordenite, mesolite, natrolite, chalcedony, chlorite/smectite, prehnite, early calcite.
- **Forms BEFORE:** Late calcite, quartz (amethystine, if present), powellite.
- **Commonly associated with:** Stilbite, heulandite, scolecite, prehnite, calcite, chalcedony, quartz, natrolite, mesolite, laumontite, chlorite, celadonite, hematite (inclusions).

### Fluorescence
- **Under SW (254 nm):** Greenish-white to yellow-green (moderate to bright).
- **Under MW (310 nm):** Yellow-green to white.
- **Under LW (365 nm):** Yellowish-white to pale green (often weaker than SW).
- **Activator:** Uranyl ion (UO₂²⁺) trace substitution; TiO₆-related activation in some green varieties.

### Famous Localities
- **Jalgaon / Nashik / Pune districts, Maharashtra, India:** Premier worldwide source; crystals to 20+ cm.
- **Berufjörður, Iceland:** Prismatic crystals with scolecite.
- **Kongsberg, Norway:** Colorless to white crystals in diabase.

## Pectolite

*Note: Pectolite is not a zeolite, but it shares the Na–Ca–Si–OH chemical environment and competes with zeolites for vug space in basalt-hosted cavities.*

### Identity
- **Formula:** NaCa₂Si₃O₈(OH) (simplified)
- **Crystal system:** Triclinic
- **Mineral group:** Inosilicate (chain silicate) — wollastonite group
- **Hardness (Mohs):** 4.5–5
- **Specific gravity:** 2.7–2.9
- **Cleavage:** Perfect on {100} and {001}
- **Fracture:** Uneven, splintery
- **Luster:** Silky to subvitreous
- **Color:** White, gray, colorless; yellowish when iron-stained; blue in Cu-substituted “larimar” variety.
- **Transparency:** Translucent to opaque
- **Streak:** White
- **Notable visual features:** Extremely slender acicular crystals in radiating “sea urchin” or “shaving brush” aggregates; fragile fibrous texture.

### Formation Conditions
- **Nucleation temperature range:** 50–250 °C
- **Optimal growth temperature:** 100–200 °C
- **Chemistry required:** Na (≥500 ppm), Ca (≥1000 ppm), Si (≥2000 ppm)
- **Required pH range:** 7.5–9.5
- **Geological environment:** Basalt vugs and vesicles; low-temperature hydrothermal veins; alpine clefts; skarns (rare).

### Paragenesis
- **Forms AFTER:** Basalt cooling; often after earlier zeolites (natrolite, thomsonite).
- **Forms BEFORE:** Later calcite, quartz, secondary copper minerals (in larimar localities).
- **Commonly associated with:** Natrolite, thomsonite, calcite, datolite, prehnite, zeolites, copper minerals (in larimar occurrences).

### Fluorescence
- **Under SW (254 nm):** Pink to orange-pink (moderate to bright).
- **Under MW (310 nm):** Orange-pink.
- **Under LW (365 nm):** Orange-pink to yellowish.
- **Activator:** Mn²⁺ trace substitution; possibly REE.

### Famous Localities
- **Jeffrey Mine, Asbestos, Quebec, Canada:** World-class radiating acicular sprays to 15 cm.
- **Paterson / Great Notch, New Jersey, USA:** Classic US locality with prehnite and datolite.
- **Sierra de Bahoruco, Dominican Republic:** Larimar — the only known blue pectolite locality.

## Deccan Traps Paragenetic Sequence

Based on Ottens et al. 2019 (*MDPI Minerals*) and field observations:

### Stage I — Near-Surface Cooling (post-eruption)
- Clay minerals: celadonite, smectite
- Early silica: chalcedony

### Stage II — Burial Metamorphism (subsequent lava flows)
- Chlorite / smectite mixed-layer clays
- **Zeolite generation I:** mordenite → heulandite → stilbite → scolecite → natrolite
  - Temperature-dependent zoning: scolecite in lower/hotter zones; heulandite in upper/cooler zones.
- Chalcedony (secondary generation)
- **Zeolite generation II:** mesolite (intermediate Na–Ca), thomsonite (Ca-rich, earlier/hotter), natrolite (Na-rich, later/cooler)

### Stage III — Late Hydrothermal
- Calcite (late generation)
- **Apophyllite** — LAST major crystalline phase
- Powellite (rare, late)

**Age context:** Apophyllite in the Deccan Traps formed 21–58 million years after the basalt erupted (~65 Ma), spanning Paleogene to early Miocene. Even within a single lava flow, different localities crystallized at different times.

## Simulator Parameters (Consolidated)

For basalt-vesicle simulation, the following environmental conditions are broadly applicable to the zeolite–apophyllite suite:

| Parameter | Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Temperature | 20–250 °C | Lower for apophyllite (20–150 °C); higher for early zeolites (up to 250 °C) |
| pH | 7.5–11 | Strongly alkaline for natrolite; mildly alkaline for scolecite/apophyllite |
| Eh | Mildly reducing to oxidizing | Not strongly redox-dependent for most zeolites |
| Si concentration | High (>2000 ppm) | Required by all species |
| Ca concentration | Moderate to high | Dominant for scolecite, thomsonite, apophyllite |
| Na concentration | Moderate to high | Dominant for natrolite, mesolite |
| Al concentration | Moderate | Suppresses pectolite/apophyllite if excessively high |
| K concentration | Low to moderate | Required for apophyllite; low in most zeolites |
| Vug fill threshold | >0.6 for apophyllite | Apophyllite nucleates only after substantial prior filling |
